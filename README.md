# NetFoundry Contributor License Agreement

Before contributing to any **NetFoundry**, **OpenZiti**, or **OpenZiti-Test-Kitchen** repository, you must sign our Contributor License Agreement (CLA).

## Why a CLA?

The CLA ensures that:
- You have the right to make your contribution
- NetFoundry can continue to distribute the projects under their open source licenses
- Your contribution can be used by the community

You only need to sign once, and it covers all repositories across our organizations.

## Individual Contributors

Signing is easy and fully automated:

1. **Open a pull request** to any of our repositories
2. **Read the CLA** - [NetFoundry Individual CLA (PDF)][ICLA]
3. **Post a comment** on your PR with this exact text:
   ```
   I have read the CLA Document and I hereby sign the CLA
   ```
4. **Done!** The bot records your signature and your PR is unblocked

### Already Signed?

Check [signatures/cla.json](./signatures/cla.json) to see if your GitHub username is listed.

## Corporate Contributors

If you're contributing on behalf of your employer, your company needs to sign a Corporate CLA:

1. Review the [NetFoundry Corporate CLA (PDF)][CCLA]
2. Have an authorized representative complete and sign it
3. Email the signed agreement to **cla@netfoundry.io**
4. Include GitHub usernames of employees authorized to contribute

We'll add your team to our allowlist within a few business days.

## Repositories Covered

This CLA applies to all repositories in:

- [github.com/netfoundry](https://github.com/netfoundry)
- [github.com/openziti](https://github.com/openziti)
- [github.com/openziti-test-kitchen](https://github.com/openziti-test-kitchen)

## Questions?

- Open an [issue](https://github.com/netfoundry/cla/issues) in this repository
- Email us at cla@netfoundry.io
